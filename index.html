WHO IS TRAILHEAD CHURCH?

God has shown us tremendous grace. Which is why in 2008, we planted a church here in Edwardsville - to share God’s love and His message of redemption through Christ. We are rooted in that grace - that underserved generosity - and from there, we move out as a community on mission, working to grow God’s kingdom and bring glory to His name.

Our core values, Truth, Community, Worship , Prayer and Mission, are informed by those of the first Christian church, as described in Acts 2:42 and 46. Trailhead Church can be described in the following statement: “Walking in Christ as a Community on Mission.”

At Trailhead Church, we believe that all who follow Christ are called to a mission of grace. In order to fulfill that mission, we must be a generous people, a sent people, and a rooted people, and that’s what this campaign is all about.

A GENEROUS PEOPLE

It is our desire that the people of Trailhead Church be the most generous and joyful people in our city - we want to see our town and our neighbors prosper through our efforts. We want generosity because we have experienced life-changing grace through Jesus Christ. Jesus was overwhelmingly generous with us; He gave up His glory, His authority, and His life so that we who are sinners and had no hope could be richly blessed with eternal joy and life. Once we experience Christ’s generosity toward us, we are compelled to generous and sacrificial giving. We give of our time, money, possessions, and ultimately, we offer our neighbors the same hope in Christ that we have been given.

A ROOTED PEOPLE

While the vision for Trailhead is to equip and send God’s people to engage their communities with the gospel, the church must also be rooted in our own community. Trailhead has the opportunity to purchase and renovate the historic First Presbyterian Church here in Edwardsville to be used as a tool for mission. It will be a place where our congregation can be changed by the gospel and equipped to live in ways that glorify and proclaim Christ, where church planters can be trained and supported, and where we host events that will support the prosperity of our community and meet the needs of our neighbors.

A SENT PEOPLE

We believe that starting new churches is one of the most effective ways to spread the gospel of Christ, and we want to see new churches spring up throughout our region, nation, and world. Trailhead is a church that was planted by another church. Church planting is in our DNA. Already a member of two networks, Acts 29 and Converge, Trailhead plans to become an equipping center to train and support aspiring church-planting networks.

An important step in equipping Trailhead to be on mission is finding a home of our own here in Edwardsville. The Bible reminds us that the church is not a building - it’s the community of Christ-followers. But a building can be a criti- cal tool for Gospel advancement. It can provide the necessary foundation for serving this community and building relationships for the sake of the Gospel.

It appears that First Presbyterian Church at 237 N. Kansas Street has the greatest potential for Trailhead. The building’s size, character, and location lend itself well to the specific needs of our church and present a number of opportunities for outreach.

Highlights of First Presbyterian’s facilities include:

  • A Gathering Place for Trailhead:
    A home of our own not only for the Sunday morning gathering but also for the family of Trailhead to host important events such as weddings, funerals, retreats, workshops, baby showers, membership classes, etc.
  • A Launchpad for Local Mission:
    A place to engage our community by hosting public concerts, lectures, forums and special events like “Affordable Christmas.”
  • A Place for Supporting Regional and National Mission:
    Space to host regional and national conferences and training sessions for church planters, missionaries, pastors, and lay leaders.
  • Current sanctuary holds 250, simple renovations would add seating for an additional 50-60
  • 90-year old sacred space with history and character
  • Located in established neighborhood near current location
  • Freedom to use the space and parking lot at any time
  • Large fellowship hall, kitchen space, offices, multiple restrooms
  • 3-story church building built in 1923
  • 1 ½ -story classroom building built in 1960
  • 4-bedroom house next church built in 1950
  • 20,729 square feet of combined building space in the three buildings
  • 7 classroom spaces for Trailhead Kids and Student Ministry
  • 1.3 acres, including 2 parking lots and a 58’ x 132’ grassy area with gazebo

Our goal is to purchase and renovate the First Presbyterian building and to raise $550,000 over 3 Years through pledges from Trailhead Church’s members and friends.

Where will the money go?

  • $85,000 for down payment on building
  • $300,000 for repairs, renovations and updates
  • $110,000 toward paying down the note
  • $55,000 for church-planting
  • $550,000 Fund-Raising Goal
